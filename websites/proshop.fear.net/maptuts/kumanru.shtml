THE ALTAR OF KUMANRU

The Altar Of Kumanru -- By Shmitz

Welcome to the Map tutorial section, where you will learn the secrets of all of your favorite Action Quake2 levels. This is the first map I will be doing in the section, but expect many, many more. Now, on with the show!

Kumanru is a small level, resembling a monastery or burial ground of sorts. It is very enclosed, but the many hallways and elaborate sewer systems provide fluid movement throughout the map. Several tunnels and hallways connect the rooms, ranging from a burial tomb, an M4 wielding altar, a sewer, and an outdoor alcove overlooked by two small sniper towers.

 


First, let's cover the sniping. While there are not many sniping opprotunities on this map, there are some. The sniper rifle is located in the tower in the upper-left of figure K-1. There is also a sniper rifle behind this point of view. If you're going to be sniping, I suggest the fortified tower, shown in figure K-2. A good strategy is to wait for a player to move towards the darkened arch. If you see an enemy head toward the doorway, put your crosshair where they will eventually be, and let them do the work for you. If they walk into your crosshair (which is almost a guarantee), squeeze a shot off.

Also, dont forget about those dirty spawn kills. There is a player-spawn point in the lower left of figure K-1. But be careful -- you tick enough people off, they'll hunt for you.

Here's the sniper tower, looking into the bell room. Those slabs swing back and forth -- which should prove to be your primary defense. Wait until an adversary attempts to walk across the ledge, and pick him off with your sniper rifle. Also, there is a ladder on the far left ladge. Ladders provide an easy shot on an unexpecting fool. Just plant your crosshair att he top of the ladder, and calmy remove the better half of his head when it floats into your crosshair.

This is a very critical room in the flow of the map. Under your perch gives players access to the outdoor area in Figure K-1, and the ladder leads you to a clip bearing room, with an elevator. Also, to the left of K-3, is a small ramp leading up from the altar room, shown in figure K-4. Watch the ramp -- it isn't traveled quite as often as the ladder, but this tends to be one of the favored grenade chucking locations. A player, grabbing the grenade from the altar room, will rush into the bell tower and drop his explosive friend, usually pulling off a kill or three.

Another warning: This tower has a back-entrance. Right outside the door under your sniper cove is a series of dirt steps and a grenade. They are quite high, and difficult to make while supressing fire, but if an enemy manages to get to the top, you'd better run -- fast. The grenadier tends to stand on the top ledge and casually toss a grenade in. This usually gets them a kill. Keep that in mind.


This is another big-action room. Keep your eyes peeled in this room. It's not as crowded as some of the others, but the M4 holder tends to hang around here. Nearby are a grenade and several pistol clips, along with a few suprises: There is a ladder in this room that brings you up to the sniper cove on the far left corner of Figure K-1, and a "secret" hatch that reveals the hand-cannon and sewer access. It is slighty shaded differently then the other walls, so you'll see it. The altar room has a hole that you can dive down in an emergency, as well, planting you safely in the sewer water below.

A good strategy for this map, is to grab the handcannon from the secret room, snag the shells, and retreat back to this room. Slowly sneak around the room, sneaking up on fools looking for the M4. Weave in and out of the pillars, looking for victims. You can usually get a frag or two just standing in the secret room, waiting for an unsuspecting sap to saunter in, and fill his upper body with buckshot.

I don't recommend trying to snipe from the tower mentioned earlier. The ladder in this room gives easy access, and there is little cover. A pistol user could easily duck and be doen with you from the ground, or he could simply go around without much trouble.

The eerily tinted sewer system... shown in figure K-5. This location is campers top choice. One of the top reasons for this is the multitude of spawn points down here. A favorite sniper/camper location is the water in the foreground of the K-5 screenshot. Usually waiting for a melee fighter to dive into the water for cover, they sit, waiting with their SSG 3000, waiting for the victim to swim into the small water straight-away. Underwater makes you very immobile while trying to duck and maneuver, and the sniper relies on this. Your best defense is simple: stay out of the water.

That may seem a little too cautious. It is. Just remember not to dive headfirst into water without checking your six.

Also, beware. The sewer access mentioned in figure the K-4 description plants you right in front of the sniper location. Snipers love this... you'll hate it. Think twice before jumping down that hole. It's a risk.
